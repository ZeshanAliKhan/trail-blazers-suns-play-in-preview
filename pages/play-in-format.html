Play-In format

What the 7-vs-8 Play-In game means.

Portland at Phoenix is listed as the West 7-vs-8 Play-In matchup. That makes the game valuable, but not final for the loser.

  1. Winner advances. The winner of the 7-vs-8 game claims the conference's No. 7 playoff seed and moves into the first round.
  2. Loser gets another game. The loser does not go home immediately. It plays the winner of the 9-vs-10 matchup for the No. 8 seed.
  3. Pressure still matters. Losing adds another must-win game, another scouting turnaround, and another chance for injuries or foul trouble to swing the season.

Why this changes the matchup

The format rewards control. Phoenix can turn a home win into a cleaner playoff entry. Portland can turn one strong road night into a direct playoff place. That is why this game should not feel casual even though the loser remains alive.

If Phoenix wins

The Suns avoid the second Play-In game and move forward with the No. 7 seed path.

If Portland wins

The Blazers take the road win and step directly into the first round.
